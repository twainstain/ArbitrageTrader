## Main Points In The Video

### What Crypto Arbitrage Means Here

The speaker defines crypto arbitrage as:

- buying where a token is cheaper
- selling or hedging where the same token is priced higher
- profiting from the spread rather than market direction

The emphasis is on **nondirectional trading** rather than prediction.

### Why A Scanner Is Useful

The speaker argues that manually checking many exchanges is unrealistic because:

- spreads move fast
- you would need many tabs open
- constant monitoring is hard to maintain

So the role of the tool is to scan many venues and surface opportunities quickly.

### The Example Trade

The video gives one concrete example:

- a price discrepancy between **Gate** and **Bybit**
- a spread on `HUSDT`
- a long opened on the cheaper venue
- a short opened on the more expensive venue
- the trade held until prices converged

The speaker claims this produced about `$6,500` in roughly one hour.

This should be treated as an example from the speaker, not as typical expected performance.

### The Three Main Advantages Claimed

The video highlights three reasons the speaker uses the tool:

1. **Speed and coverage**
2. **Manual capital control**
3. **Structured opportunity types**

Expanded:

- it scans CEX and DEX spreads
- it does not require the tool to directly control exchange funds
- it helps surface different arbitrage structures, not just random price differences

### Spot-Futures Convergence

The speaker describes one favorite strategy:

- buy spot on one exchange
- short futures on another
- wait for spread convergence

The framing is that this can work in:

- bull markets
- bear markets
- sideways markets

because the goal is convergence, not directional prediction.

### Funding Rate Arbitrage

The video also discusses funding-rate arbitrage:

- when funding is highly positive, shorts get paid
- when funding is highly negative, longs get paid
- traders can structure positions to collect funding while reducing directional exposure

This is presented as another reason the tool is useful.
